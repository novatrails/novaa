The Roof of Africa

Mount Kilimanjaro stands majestically as Africa's highest peak and the world's tallest free-standing mountain. This iconic stratovolcano offers adventurers the unique opportunity to trek through five distinct climate zones, from tropical rainforest to arctic summit.

With multiple routes to the summit, Kilimanjaro welcomes climbers of various skill levels. Each path offers its own unique perspective and challenges, leading to the ultimate reward: standing atop Uhuru Peak at 5,895 meters above sea level.

Experience the journey of a lifetime as you traverse diverse landscapes, witness stunning sunrises above the clouds, and achieve one of the world's most coveted mountaineering goals.

Kilimanjaro Routes

Choose the perfect path to the summit based on your experience and preferences

Machame Route

Moderate

The "Whiskey Route" is the most popular and scenic path to the summit, offering diverse landscapes and excellent acclimatization.

  • 6-7 days duration
  • High success rate (85%)
  • Scenic Barranco Wall climb
  • Varied landscapes
  • Good acclimatization profile
From $2,800 per person

Lemosho Route

Easy-Moderate

The most scenic and pristine route with excellent acclimatization opportunities and lower traffic on the early stages.

  • 7-8 days duration
  • Highest success rate (90%)
  • Remote wilderness experience
  • Excellent acclimatization
  • Beautiful Shira Plateau
From $3,200 per person

Marangu Route

Easy

The "Coca-Cola Route" offers hut accommodation and is considered the easiest path, perfect for beginners.

  • 5-6 days duration
  • Hut accommodation
  • Less physically demanding
  • Good for beginners
  • Same ascent/descent route
From $2,400 per person

Rongai Route

Moderate

The only route approaching from the north, offering a unique perspective and fewer crowds.

  • 6-7 days duration
  • Less crowded
  • Drier conditions
  • Wildlife viewing opportunities
  • Gentler gradient
From $2,600 per person

Northern Circuit

Moderate

The longest and newest route offering the most comprehensive Kilimanjaro experience with excellent acclimatization.

  • 8-9 days duration
  • Highest success rate (95%)
  • 360-degree mountain views
  • Excellent acclimatization
  • Remote northern slopes
From $3,800 per person

Umbwe Route

Challenging

The steepest and most direct route, designed for experienced trekkers seeking a challenging adventure.

  • 6-7 days duration
  • Steepest ascent
  • Most challenging route
  • Dramatic ridge walk
  • For experienced climbers
From $2,900 per person

Summit Success Tips

Expert advice to maximize your chances of reaching Uhuru Peak

1

Choose the Right Route

Select a route that matches your experience level and allows adequate time for acclimatization. Longer routes generally have higher success rates.

2

Train Consistently

Build endurance through regular cardio exercise, hiking with a loaded pack, and strength training focusing on legs and core muscles.

3

Hydrate & Nutrition

Drink plenty of water (3-4 liters daily), eat regularly even when appetite decreases, and avoid alcohol during the climb.

4

Listen to Your Guide

Follow your guide's pace and advice. "Pole pole" (slowly slowly) is key to successful acclimatization and summit success.

5

Layer Your Clothing

Use the layering system to regulate body temperature as you move through different climate zones and weather conditions.

6

Stay Positive

Maintain a positive mental attitude, celebrate small victories, and remember that summit day is just the beginning of your descent journey.
